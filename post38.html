Revenue Metrics

Know your money.

ARR
Annual Recurring Revenue. Total yearly revenue from active subscriptions. The north star metric for every SaaS.
ARR = MRR × 12.
MRR
Monthly Recurring Revenue. What customers pay you every month. Excludes one-time fees, services, and setup charges. Only predictable, recurring income.
ARPU
Average Revenue Per User. Total revenue divided by total customers. Tells you how much each customer is worth. Rising ARPU = healthy upsell motion.
Burn Rate
Cash spent per month. If you have $5M in the bank and burn $250K/month, you have 20 months of runway. Investors ask this first.
Burn Multiple
Net burn ÷ net new ARR. Measures how efficiently you turn spending into growth. Below 2× is great. Above 4× means you're burning cash without enough return.

Retention & Growth

Keep your customers.

Churn
Percentage of customers or revenue lost per month/year. 5% monthly churn = 46% annual loss. The silent killer of every SaaS business.
NDR
Net Dollar Retention. Revenue from existing customers after expansion, contraction, and churn. Above 100% = you grow even without new sales. Elite is 120%+.
GDR
Gross Dollar Retention. Same as NDR but without expansion. Pure retention. Measures how much revenue you keep. Max is 100%. Below 85% is a red flag.
DAU/MAU
Daily Active Users ÷ Monthly Active Users. Measures product stickiness. Above 40% = daily habit (like Slack). Below 15% = occasional use.
Activation
% of signups who reach the "aha moment." If users don't activate, they churn. Improving activation from 20% to 40% doubles your effective growth rate overnight.

Efficiency & Unit Economics

Make the math work.

LTV
Customer Lifetime Value. Total revenue a customer generates before churning. LTV = ARPU × gross margin ÷ churn rate. The ceiling of what you can spend to acquire them.
CAC
Customer Acquisition Cost. Total sales + marketing spend ÷ new customers acquired. The floor of your growth investment. Must be far below LTV.
LTV/CAC
The efficiency ratio. Must be 3× or higher. Below 3× = burning cash. Above 5× = underinvesting in growth. Sweet spot is 3-5×.
CAC Payback
Months to recover acquisition cost. Under 12 months = healthy. Over 18 = cash flow problem. Determines how fast you can reinvest in growth.
Rule of 40
Growth rate + profit margin ≥ 40%. The single number VCs use to evaluate SaaS health. Growing 60% at -20% margins? 60 + (-20) = 40. You pass.
Gross Margin
Revenue minus cost of delivery. SaaS should be 70-85%. Below 60% means your infrastructure costs are too high or you're selling services, not software.

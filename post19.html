04 / 07ravenex.tech

TRAINED
NATIVELY
AT 1.58 BITS.

This is NOT post-training quantization (like GPTQ/AWQ). BitNet trains from scratch with ternary weights. The model learns to compensate for reduced precision from day one.

OLD
Post-training quantization (PTQ)Train in FP16 → compress to INT8/INT4 after training → accuracy degrades, especially below 4 bits. Retrofitting precision onto an architecture designed for floats.
NEW
Quantization-aware training (BitNet)Replace nn.Linear with BitLinear → train directly with ternary constraints → model learns to work with -1/0/+1 from the start. No precision loss because there's nothing to lose.

The key insight: The BitLinear layer uses absmean quantization: divide each weight by the mean of absolute values, then round to nearest of {-1, 0, +1}. Activations stay at 8-bit. This preserves enough signal for the model to learn rich representations with ternary weights.

Why addition matters: On modern CPUs, multiplication consumes ~30× more energy than addition. BitNet replaces every single multiplication in the forward pass with addition or subtraction. At 100B parameters, that's trillions of multiplications eliminated per inference.

MAKE THE
MATH FUZZIER.
KEEP THE BRAIN.

What BitNet proves: Neural networks don't need 32-bit precision. They don't need 16. They don't even need 4. Three values — subtract, skip, add — are enough for a model to learn language, math, and reasoning. The information lives in the connections, not the precision of individual weights.

What's coming:

• BitNet distillation: convert existing FP16 models to 1.58-bit without retraining from scratch (10× memory savings, 2.65× faster)

• Custom BitNet hardware: chips with native ternary ALUs (no floating-point units needed)

• Edge AI: trillion-parameter reasoning on phones, cars, glasses

• TII's Falcon adopted BitNet architecture for their next generation

The 1-bit revolution isn't about making AI worse. It's about discovering how little precision AI actually needs and using that discovery to put intelligence everywhere.

02 / 07

DevOps Works.
Then It Doesn't.

Promise
"You build it, you run it." Teams own features and production. No ops silos, no tickets to a separate team. Fast, accountable, no wall to throw code over.
Startup
10 engineers, one codebase, one cluster. DevOps ownership works perfectly here. Teams move fast and understand their own systems end to end.
Growth
200 engineers, 40 services, 15 teams. Each team now owns Kubernetes, Terraform, observability, on-call, security, and cost management simultaneously.
Result
Cognitive load becomes unbearable. Developers writing features are also expected to write Helm charts, configure PagerDuty, tune Prometheus, and understand blast radius.

03 / 07

The Cognitive Load
Problem.

Owned by all
Every team owns: features, infra manifests, CI pipelines, observability config, security policies, on-call rotations, and cloud costs. None of these are small.
Toil cost
30 to 40% of developer time spent on operational plumbing instead of product value at companies without a platform team.
Onboarding
A new engineer spends weeks learning which Terraform modules, Grafana dashboards, and CI templates are the approved ones. No single source of truth.
Duplication
15 teams write 15 versions of the same Kubernetes deployment pattern, alerting rule, and incident runbook. None interoperate. All drift over time.

04 / 07

The Internal
Developer Platform.

IDP
A curated layer of tooling that abstracts infrastructure complexity. Create a service, get infrastructure, CI, monitoring, and on-call routing provisioned automatically.
Golden paths
Opinionated, well-supported routes through the developer experience. Not mandated. Just so much easier than the alternative that teams adopt them without being asked.
Self-service
A production-ready service in minutes, not days. The platform team encodes the decisions so individual teams do not need to make them every time.
Backstage
Spotify's open-source developer portal. Catalogs every service, its owners, dependencies, and deployment status. Adopted by Google, Netflix, Airbnb, Zalando.

06 / 07

DORA
Metrics.

Deploy Freq
How often a team ships to production. Elite teams deploy multiple times per day. Platform golden paths reduce friction on every single deploy.
Lead Time
Time from commit to production. Platform tooling cuts this by eliminating manual Terraform, Helm, and pipeline setup steps per new service.
MTTR
Mean Time to Recovery. Standardized dashboards and runbooks built into the platform make every incident faster to resolve across all teams.
Change Fail
Percentage of deploys causing production incidents. Shared CI templates with mandatory test gates reduce this rate simultaneously across every team.

Quick
Reference.

Platf. Eng.
Discipline focused on building reusable internal tooling that reduces developer cognitive load while preserving team ownership and accountability.
IDP
Internal Developer Platform. Provisions infrastructure, CI, monitoring, and on-call as a self-service workflow. The product a platform team builds and maintains.
Golden Path
Opinionated, well-supported route through the developer experience. Not mandatory. Clearly the easiest and most correct way to do things.
Backstage
Spotify's open-source developer portal. De facto standard for IDP catalog layers. Catalogs services, owners, dependencies, and operational status.
DORA
Deployment Frequency, Lead Time, MTTR, Change Failure Rate. Four research-backed measures of software delivery performance. Platform quality shows up in all four.

Caption
The "you build it, you run it" principle was right. And it broke everything. DevOps promised to close the gap between engineering and operations. Give developers ownership of production. No more tickets to a separate ops team. It worked at startup scale: 10 engineers, one codebase, clear accountability. Teams moved fast and understood their systems. Then those teams multiplied. The cognitive load became unbearable. A developer at a growing company was expected to write features, write tests, write Kubernetes manifests, configure monitoring, be on call at 3am, manage database migrations, audit security policies, and review cloud costs. None of these are small things. Each is a specialty. DevOps asked every engineer to own all of them simultaneously, every sprint. Platform Engineering is not a rejection of DevOps ownership. It is a recognition that ownership works best when friction is removed. The platform team builds an internal developer platform, a curated set of golden paths that encode best practices into self-service tooling. A developer creates a new service not by reading 40 wiki pages, but by running one command that provisions infrastructure, configures CI, sets up monitoring, and opens a pull request. The platform team treats developers as customers. They measure success the same way product teams do, with DORA metrics. Deployment frequency, lead time for changes, mean time to recovery, and change failure rate. A good platform makes all four better by getting out of the developer's way. Backstage, the developer portal open-sourced by Spotify, is now the standard for internal platforms. A service catalog entry declares what a service is and who owns it. The platform reads it and wires up the rest automatically. The infrastructure did not get simpler. The developer experience did.

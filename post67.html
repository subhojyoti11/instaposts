Why Provenance
Matters Now.

Definition
Data provenance is the complete record of where data originated, who transformed it, and how it was used. It answers: where did this come from, and how did it get here?
Before AI
Provenance mattered for compliance and auditing. Knowing which database row came from which source was important for regulated industries, but optional for most teams outside of finance and healthcare.
After AI
Training data provenance now affects legal liability, copyright exposure, and model safety. If a model was trained on data collected without consent, every inference it produces carries that risk forward into production.
The gap
Most organizations cannot answer "what was in our training data and where did it come from?" The teams that trained the model may no longer work there. The dataset was assembled from dozens of sources with inconsistent documentation.

03 / 07

The Lineage
Problem.

Source
Data arrives from web scrapes, licensed datasets, internal databases, and third-party APIs. Each source has different terms of service, consent frameworks, and geographic jurisdiction. Mixing them without tracking creates legal exposure.
Transform
Raw data passes through cleaning, deduplication, filtering, and augmentation pipelines. Each step changes the dataset. Without a full transformation log, the relationship between the original source and the training set is broken.
Version
Training datasets change between model versions. Knowing exactly which version of the dataset produced which model is required for reproducibility, debugging, and regulatory audits in regulated domains.
Deletion
GDPR and similar regulations grant users the right to erasure. If a user's data was in the training set, the organization must know which model checkpoints were trained on it and whether retraining is required upon deletion.

05 / 07

Legal and
Compliance Pressure.

EU AI Act
The EU AI Act requires high-risk AI systems to document training data sources, including copyright status and data collection methods. Non-compliance carries fines up to 3% of global annual revenue.
Copyright suits
Multiple lawsuits against AI companies allege that training on copyrighted content without a license constitutes infringement. Organizations that cannot document their training data sources face litigation risk on every model they deploy.
GDPR erasure
If a model memorized personally identifiable information from training data, a deletion request may require retraining. Without provenance records, the organization cannot determine which models are affected or the scope of remediation.
Model cards
Hugging Face model cards and Google's Data Cards are emerging standards for documenting training data provenance. Enterprise AI governance frameworks now require these artifacts before production deployment approval.

06 / 07

In
Production.

DVC
Data Version Control tracks dataset versions alongside model versions in Git. Every experiment references a specific dataset hash. Reproducing a training run requires only the commit hash and DVC remote pointer.
MLflow
MLflow logs dataset metadata as part of every training run. The run record links the model artifact to the exact dataset version, parameters, and environment used to produce it. Full lineage is queryable by run ID.
DataHub
LinkedIn open-sourced DataHub as a data catalog with lineage tracking. It visualizes the full graph from raw source tables through transformation pipelines to the final datasets consumed by models in production.
OpenLineage
An open standard for lineage metadata, supported by Airflow, Spark, dbt, and Flink. Pipeline runs emit lineage events that tools like Marquez collect and visualize, providing a vendor-neutral provenance record across the stack.

07 / 07

Quick
Reference.

Provenance
The complete record of where data originated, who transformed it, and which models it trained. Required for regulatory compliance, copyright defense, GDPR erasure requests, and model reproducibility audits.
Lineage
The graph of transformations connecting raw source data to a final dataset or model artifact. Lineage tools like OpenLineage and DataHub capture this graph automatically as pipelines run.
DVC
Data Version Control: tracks dataset versions in Git with content hashes. Ensures that a specific model checkpoint can be reproduced from the same data and code, even years after the original training run.
Model card
Structured documentation of a model's training data, intended use, limitations, and evaluation results. Required by EU AI Act for high-risk systems. Standard artifact in enterprise AI governance review processes.
Right to erasure
GDPR Article 17 grants individuals the right to have their personal data deleted. If that data was in a training set, the organization must trace which models were trained on it and evaluate whether retraining is required.

Most AI teams cannot answer the question: where did our training data come from? Data provenance is the complete record of where data originated, who transformed it, and how it was used. In traditional data engineering this was a compliance requirement for regulated industries. In the AI era it has become a prerequisite for operating legally and safely at scale. The problem is structural. Training datasets are assembled from dozens of sources: web scrapes, licensed feeds, internal databases, and third-party APIs. Each source has different terms of service, consent frameworks, and jurisdictional rules. Data passes through cleaning, deduplication, PII scrubbing, and filtering pipelines. Each transformation changes the dataset, and without a full log of those transformations, the connection between the original source and the model's training set is broken. This matters for three concrete reasons. First, copyright: multiple ongoing lawsuits allege that training on copyrighted content without a license constitutes infringement. Organizations that cannot document their data sources cannot defend against these claims. Second, GDPR erasure: if a user's personal data was in the training set, the organization must know which models trained on it and whether retraining is required upon deletion. Third, the EU AI Act requires high-risk AI systems to document training data sources, including copyright status, or face fines up to 3% of global annual revenue. DVC (Data Version Control) tracks dataset versions alongside code in Git so any training run can be reproduced years later. MLflow links every model artifact to the exact dataset version that produced it. OpenLineage is an open standard for emitting lineage events from Airflow, Spark, and dbt pipelines, captured in tools like Marquez and DataHub. Provenance is not bureaucratic overhead. It is the chain of custody for one of your organization's most valuable assets.

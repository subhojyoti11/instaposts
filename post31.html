Every word
looks at
every other word.

"The cat sat on the mat because it was tired."

What does "it" refer to? The cat or the mat? Humans know instantly. Previous AI models couldn't figure this out reliably.

Attention solves this by letting every word compute a relevance score with every other word. "it" attends strongly to "cat" (high score) and weakly to "mat" (low score). The model learns these patterns from billions of examples.

Self-attention — means the sentence attends to itself. Each word asks: "Which other words in this sentence are relevant to understanding ME?" Then it adjusts its representation based on the answer.

Query, Key,
Value.

Three matrices. That's the entire mechanism.

For each token, create 3 vectors:
──────────────────────────
Q (Query): "What am I looking for?"
K (Key): "What do I contain?"
V (Value): "What info do I carry?"
──────────────────────────
Step 1: Q × K^T = attention scores
"How relevant is each word to me?"
Step 2: softmax(scores) = weights
Normalize to probabilities (sum = 1)
Step 3: weights × V = output
Blend the Values by relevance

Library analogy — Q is your search query. K is the label on each book. V is the content inside. You match your query against all labels (Q×K), then read the most relevant books (weighted V).

One
equation.

Attention(Q,K,V) = softmax(QK^T / √d_k) × V
──────────────────────────
Q × K^T
Dot product of every Query with every Key
Creates an N×N attention matrix
(N = number of tokens)
──────────────────────────
÷ √d_k
Scale by square root of key dimension
Prevents dot products from getting too large
Without this, softmax saturates → no gradients
──────────────────────────
softmax
Convert scores to probabilities (0 to 1)
──────────────────────────
× V
Weighted sum of Value vectors

Why multiple
heads?

One attention head can only learn one type of relationship. Multiple heads learn different patterns simultaneously.

H1
Syntactic head. Learns subject-verb agreement. "The dogs [that the boy feeds] are happy" — links "dogs" to "are"
H2
Coreference head. Learns pronoun resolution. "Sarah told John she was leaving" — links "she" to "Sarah"
H3
Positional head. Tracks word distance. Adjacent words, sentence boundaries, paragraph structure
H4
Semantic head. Groups related concepts. "Doctor prescribed medicine for the patient" — clusters medical terms

GPT-4: 96 attention heads per layer × 120 layers = 11,520 attention operations for every token. Each one learning a different linguistic relationship.

Attention is
O(n²).

Every token attends to every other token. Double the context length? Quadruple the compute.

Tokens Attention operations
──────────────────────────
1K 1,000,000 (1M)
4K 16,000,000 (16M)
32K 1,024,000,000 (1B)
128K 16,384,000,000 (16B)
1M 1,000,000,000,000 (1T)
──────────────────────────
1M context = 1 TRILLION operations.
Per layer. Per head. Per token generated.

This is why long context is expensive. Flash Attention, Ring Attention, and sparse attention all exist to reduce this O(n²) bottleneck. Without these optimizations, 1M context windows would be computationally impossible.

What attention
replaced.

RNNs
Processed words sequentially. Token 100 barely "remembered" token 1. Couldn't parallelize. Training took weeks for small models
LSTMs
Added memory gates. Better at long sequences but still sequential. Maximum practical context: ~1,000 tokens
CNNs
Parallel but local. Could only see fixed-size windows (5-10 words). Missed long-range dependencies entirely

Attention changed everything: Parallel processing (train on entire sequence at once). Global context (every token sees every token). Scalable (add more layers, more heads, more data). This is why we went from BERT (2018, 110M params) to GPT-4 (2023, ~1.8T params) in 5 years.

One idea
changed
everything.

Attention is not a model. It's a mechanism. A way for neural networks to dynamically focus on relevant information. It works in language (GPT), vision (ViT), audio (Whisper), protein folding (AlphaFold), and robotics.

The key insight: Instead of processing information in a fixed order (sequentially) or fixed window (locally), let the model LEARN which parts of the input are relevant to each output. Let it attend to what matters.

The remaining challenge: O(n²) scaling. Every improvement in context length requires either more compute or a breakthrough in efficient attention (Flash Attention, linear attention, SSMs like Mamba). The race to solve this defines the next era of AI.

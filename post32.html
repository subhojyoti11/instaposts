Every word becomes a coordinate.

embed("king") → [0.21, -0.84, 0.55, ...]
embed("queen") → [0.19, -0.81, 0.58, ...]
embed("car") → [-0.72, 0.33, -0.12, ...]
──────────────────────────
Each word is a point in space.
Similar meaning = nearby points.
Different meaning = far apart.
──────────────────────────
OpenAI's text-embedding-3:
3,072 dimensions per word
A point in 3,072-dimensional space.

You can't visualize 3,072 dimensions. But the principle is the same as 2D or 3D: similar things cluster together. "Paris" is near "London" and "Tokyo." All three are far from "bicycle."

What happened?
"king" - "man" = the concept of
ROYALTY (without gender)
"royalty" + "woman" =

This isn't programmed. Nobody told the model that king-man+woman=queen. It LEARNED this structure from reading billions of sentences. The relationships are emergent. The vector space organizes itself around meaning.

How to measure similarity.

Cosine similarity = angle between vectors
──────────────────────────
cos("happy", "joyful") = 0.92 ← very similar
cos("happy", "sad") = 0.38 ← related (emotions)
cos("happy", "table") = 0.05 ← unrelated
──────────────────────────
1.0 = identical meaning
0.0 = no relationship
-1.0 = opposite meaning

Why cosine, not distance? Cosine measures direction, not magnitude. Two sentences about dogs should be similar whether they're 5 words or 500 words long. Direction captures meaning. Distance captures length.

Embeddings power everything.

RAG
Semantic search. User asks "refund policy", embeddings find the doc about "return process" even though the words differ. Meaning match, not keyword match
Rec
Recommendations. Netflix, Spotify, Amazon all embed users and items into the same space. Similar users = similar taste. Nearest items = recommendations
Clu
Clustering. Group 100,000 support tickets by meaning automatically. No labels needed. Similar tickets land near each other in vector space
Dup
Deduplication. "How do I reset my password?" and "I forgot my login credentials" have cosine similarity 0.89. Same question, different words. Merge them
Mod
Moderation. Embed toxic content examples. Flag anything with cosine > 0.8 to any example. Catches paraphrased abuse that keyword filters miss

The practical cheatsheet.

01
OpenAI text-embedding-3-small. $0.02/MTok. 1,536 dims. Good enough for 90% of use cases. Start here
02
Store in a vector database. Pinecone, Weaviate, Chroma, pgvector. This is where your embeddings live for fast retrieval
03
Chunk your documents. 200-500 tokens per chunk. Too small = no context. Too large = diluted meaning. Overlap 10-20%
04
Cosine similarity > 0.75. Below this threshold, the match is too weak to trust. Above 0.85, it's a strong match. Tune for your data

The key insight: Embeddings bridge the gap between human language and machine math. They let computers understand that "I'm starving" and "I need food" mean the same thing, without being told. This is the foundation of everything from ChatGPT to Google Search.

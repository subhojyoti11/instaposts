05

Why Meta gives it away for free.

Meta spent hundreds of millions training Llama 4. Then released the weights for free. Then launched Muse Spark, a closed model. Why both?

01
Commoditize the complement. If AI models are free, the value shifts to data and distribution. Meta has 3.9B users. They own the data moat
02
Community R&D. 500,000+ developers improve Llama for free. Fine-tunes, optimizations, bug fixes. Meta gets the innovation without paying for it
03
Two-track strategy. Llama (open) builds ecosystem + developer trust. Muse Spark (closed) captures commercial revenue. Meta wants both moats
04
Starve competitors. If Llama is free and good enough, why pay OpenAI? Every Llama deployment is one fewer GPT customer

07

The lock-in
problem.

Every closed-source API can change pricing, deprecate models, or kill products without warning.

GPT-4
Fully deprecated. Code that worked in 2023 breaks in 2026. Three generations later (4o → 5.4 → 5.5), no rollback possible
Pricing
Shifts overnight. Grok 4.3 undercuts everyone at $0.20/MTok. OpenAI responded with Nano at $0.10. Your unit economics change without warning
Sora
Shut down Apr 2026. OpenAI killed the Sora app entirely. Devs who built on it got nothing. API shutting down Sep 2026

Open source = insurance. You own the weights. Nobody can deprecate your model. Nobody can change the pricing. Nobody can read your data. That stability has real business value.

08

The smart
play is
both.

Use closed for: Cutting-edge agentic coding (Opus 4.7, GPT-5.5), rapid prototyping, low-volume high-complexity queries. When 87%+ SWE-bench matters more than cost.

Use open for: High-volume inference (DeepSeek V4, Llama 4 Scout), data-sensitive workloads (healthcare/legal/finance), fine-tuning on proprietary data. Zero vendor lock-in.

The 2026 pattern: Prototype on Claude/GPT. Validate the use case. Then distill or fine-tune DeepSeek V4 or Llama 4 for production. Best quality during development, lowest cost at scale.

Even Meta runs both now, Llama (open) + Muse Spark (closed). The future isn't open OR closed. It's both, deployed strategically.

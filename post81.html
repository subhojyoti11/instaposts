The Prompting
Problem.

What you do
Write a command, get one output, evaluate it yourself. If it misses the mark, you become the QA system.
What the model does
Generates one response and stops. No awareness of whether it met your actual intent.
The bottleneck
Complex tasks need multiple refinement cycles. Each iteration costs your time and cognitive load.
The result
Developers spend 40% of AI interaction time re-prompting to fix bad outputs. You are the loop.

03 / 07

How AI Looping
Works.

Goal not prompt
You define what perfect looks like, not what the AI should do. The goal has a success criterion the model can evaluate itself against.
Self-critique
The model evaluates its own output against your goal criterion. A fast, cheap model typically runs the critique step.
Auto re-prompt
If critique returns FAIL, the model writes its own corrective prompt and runs again. You are not in the loop.
Loop exit
The loop terminates when self-evaluation returns PASS. Typically 3-5 iterations for complex goals.

05 / 07

Prompting
vs Looping.

Who iterates
Prompting: you rewrite after evaluating the output. Looping: the model rewrites its own prompt from its self-critique.
Error detection
Prompting: you spot the mistake manually. Looping: the model spots it using a fast critique model.
Best for
Prompting: simple, one-shot tasks with clear outputs. Looping: complex, multi-step goals where correctness is verifiable.
Where it's live
OpenAI o3, Cursor Agent, Devin, Claude tool-use chains. All run internal loops before returning a final answer.

07 / 07

Quick
Reference.

AI Loop
Generate, self-critique against a goal, self-prompt to improve, repeat until PASS. The model is the QA system.
Goal
A success criterion describing what perfect output looks like. The loop runs until the model's self-evaluation passes.
Self-critique
A fast model (Haiku, GPT-4o-mini) evaluates the output of a capable model (Sonnet, GPT-4o) against the goal.
Agentic AI
AI loops combined with tool use to autonomously complete multi-step tasks. Cursor, Devin, and o3 are all agentic.
Prompt
A one-shot instruction with no self-correction. Sufficient for simple tasks. Insufficient for complex goals that require iteration.
